What is Fluoride?

Fluoride is a common name for a variety of chemicals that contain the (negative) anion of the element Fluorine.

In Australia, fluoride is added to drinking water (with chlorine) usually by adding a slurry of sodium fluorosilicate(Na 2 [SiF6]) or solution of hydrofluorosilicic acid (H 2[SiF6]). Fluoride in toothpaste is the main active ingredient and often in the form of sodium monofluorophosphate [Na2PO3F].

History of Fluoride use:

  • 3 Men most responsible

    The history of flouridation of drinking water is a tale of three men in the USA in the early 20th Century. From left to right:

    • Dr Frederick McKay
    • Dr H. Trendley Dean
    • Dr G.V. Black

  • Starting in Colorado Springs

    It all started with Dr Frederick McKay, who moved from the East Coast to open a dental practice in Colorado Springs. He quickly noticed that many of the residents had a "grotesque brown stain" on their teeth.

  • Colorado Brown Stain

    This Colorado Brown Stain affliction, was not documented before and was an unknown origin. Locals blamed everything from too much pork consumption to the calcuim rich water.

    • >

  • Enter Dr Black

    In 1909 Dr G.V. Black agreed to help Dr McKay and arrived in Colorado Springs. He was shocked by the previliance of the staining in Children. Mckay and Black discovered that this staining lead to decay resistance, but also seem to impact primarily developing teeth.

  • Further Investigations

    It still wasn't know what was causing the staining, but water was suspected. In 1923, McKay went over the Rockies to Oakley, Idaho. There he interviewed parents of children with the same staining. Water was tested but yeilded no results.

  • Dr Kempf

    Enter Dr. Grover Kempf from the US Public Health Service (PHS), who travelled with Dr McKay to Bauxite, Arkansas home to an aluminum company (ALCOA). Again children were displaying staining, but not in the towns 5 miles away. Water again tested with no results.

  • Dr H. Trendley Dean

    ALCOA even started testing samples, fearful of negative press. Using photospectrographic analysis, large amounts of flourides were dectected in the water. Finally a cause was found. Enter Dr H. Trendley Dean from the US PHS, initial research by Black and McKay and wondered about adding flouride in the water for preventing tooth decay.

  • Grand Rapids Study

    Dean wanted to implement a study to see and Grand Rapids was the first city in the world to add this public health measure in 1945. This formed a study case for over 30 years, monitoring over 30,000 school children in Grand Rapids. The study conculed that Flouridation was safe and greatly deceased tooth decay in the population (by over 60%).

  • Dr McKay's Legacy

    Many may no longer remember Dr's McKay, Black, Dean and Kempf, but together they help put in place something the CDC has called one of the greatest preventave health measures of the 20th century. McKay's determination to find the answers has saved millions of teeth from cavities around the world.

Fluoride in Drinking Water

Fluoridation of drinking water has been heralded a public health boon since the 1950s accross the western world. The small doses in drinking water improve dental enamel, strengthening it against decay. This benefit is particularly pronounced in children's developing teeth.

Safety of Fluoride

It is important to note that Fluoride is very safe when ingested in drinking water or when using toothpaste. Fluorosis can only occur when the Fluoride in the drinking water far exceeds the current standard of 1.5mg/L (15 parts per million). Flourosis will begin to occur when the amount of flouride in the drinking water is around 100 parts per million and the exposure is over years.
Even then, Fluorosis can only impact developing teeth in children and the primary affliction of the condition is staining.

Fluoride in Toothpaste

As we have noted on this site, Fluoride aids to strengthen tooth enamel. Many countries such as Australia have fluoride in the water, which helps strengthen teeth as they develop in children and improves restiatnce to bacteria and their acids in the mouth. However Flouridation of drinking water dramatically reduces the chance of cavities, it does not offer 100% protection. Fluoride in toothpaste further helps to harden and strengthen enamel as you brush your teeth.
